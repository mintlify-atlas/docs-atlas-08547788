Lidraughts includes a tactics puzzle trainer at [lidraughts.org/training](https://lidraughts.org/training). Puzzles are positions taken from real games where there is a single best sequence of moves. Solving puzzles improves your ability to spot forced captures, combinations, and winning plans during your own games.

## Finding puzzles

Visit [lidraughts.org/training](https://lidraughts.org/training) to start solving puzzles immediately — no account required. Each puzzle presents a position and asks you to find the best move or sequence.

Puzzles are available for all supported variants:

| Variant | URL |
|---|---|
| Standard | `/training` or `/training/new` |
| Frisian | `/training/frisian/new` |
| Antidraughts | `/training/antidraughts/new` |
| Frysk! | `/training/frysk/new` |
| Breakthrough | `/training/breakthrough/new` |
| Russian | `/training/russian/new` |
| Brazilian | `/training/brazilian/new` |

## The solving interface

When you open a puzzle:

1. The board is shown from the perspective of the side to move.
2. The position is reached by playing the last move of the source game automatically — you see what your opponent just played.
3. You must find and play the correct response.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Study the position">
    Look for forcing sequences: multi-captures, promotion threats, and pieces at risk.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make your move">
    Click a piece and then the destination square. If the move is correct, the opponent's reply is played automatically and you continue until the sequence is complete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Result">
    A green highlight means your move was correct. A red highlight means it was wrong — the correct move is shown and you can continue from there.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Next puzzle">
    After completing a puzzle, click **Continue** to load the next one.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You can use the board editor or analysis board alongside a puzzle to explore alternative lines. Open [lidraughts.org/analysis](https://lidraughts.org/analysis) in a separate tab and paste the FEN from the puzzle.
</Tip>

## Rating system

Puzzles and players each have a Glicko-2 rating. Solving a puzzle correctly increases your puzzle rating; failing reduces it. The puzzle's own rating adjusts in the opposite direction.

- Starting puzzle rating: 1500
- Your puzzle rating is displayed on the training page and in your profile.
- Puzzle ratings are separate from your game ratings and do not affect them.

<Info>
Because puzzles use the Glicko-2 system, your rating deviation decreases as you solve more puzzles, making your rating more stable and accurate over time.
</Info>

## Daily puzzle

The daily puzzle is available at [lidraughts.org/training/daily](https://lidraughts.org/training/daily). A single featured puzzle is selected each day based on vote ratio — higher-voted puzzles are preferred. The daily puzzle is the same for all users.

The daily puzzle is also displayed on the Lidraughts homepage for logged-in users.

## Batch solving

The batch API allows external applications to fetch and submit solutions for multiple puzzles at once:

```text
GET  /training/{variant}/batch   — fetch a batch of puzzles
POST /training/{variant}/batch   — submit solutions for a batch
```

## Voting on puzzles

After solving a puzzle you can vote it up or down:

- **Thumbs up** — the puzzle is well-constructed and instructive.
- **Thumbs down** — the puzzle is flawed, has an alternative solution, or is not instructive.

Votes affect whether a puzzle continues to appear for other users. Puzzles with a vote ratio below the minimum threshold are disabled from the normal training rotation.

To vote:

```text
POST /training/{id}/vote             (Standard)
POST /training/{variant}/{id}/vote   (other variants)
```

## Puzzle export

Puzzle positions can be exported as PNG images:

```text
GET /training/export/png/{id}.png
GET /training/export/png/{variant}/{id}.png
```
