Lidraughts maintains a directory of approved streamers who broadcast draughts content. When a listed streamer goes live, they appear at the top of the streamers page so other users can watch.

## Streamers listing

Visit [lidraughts.org/streamer](https://lidraughts.org/streamer) to see all streamers. The page is split into two sections:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Live now" icon="circle-dot">
    Streamers who are currently broadcasting appear at the top with their stream status.
  </Card>
  <Card title="All streamers" icon="list">
    Every approved streamer is listed below, even when offline, with their profile and channel information.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Streamer profile page

Each streamer has a profile page at `/streamer/<username>` showing:

- Current live status and stream details
- Recent activity on Lidraughts (last 10 activity items)
- A **Follow** button to follow the player on Lidraughts

A streamer's profile page is only accessible if the streamer is listed (approved), or if you are viewing your own page, or if you have admin access.

## Featured streamers API

The featured streamers endpoint at `/streamer/featured` returns a JSON list of currently live streams that have been automatically featured. Each entry includes:

```json
{
  "url": "https://lidraughts.org/streamer/username",
  "status": "...",
  "user": {
    "id": "username",
    "name": "DisplayName",
    "title": "IM"
  }
}
```

The `/api/streamer/live` endpoint returns a list of currently live streamers as lightweight user objects, including a `playing` flag if they are in an active game.

## Becoming a streamer

Anyone can apply for a streamer profile. The process has three steps: create your streamer record, fill in your profile, and request approval.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your streamer record">
    Log in and go to [lidraughts.org/streamer/edit](https://lidraughts.org/streamer/edit). If you have never registered as a streamer before, click **Become a streamer**. This creates your streamer record and takes you to the edit form.

    <Note>
      Users who are shadowbanned or whose accounts have been flagged as "lame" cannot register as streamers.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in your streamer profile">
    On the edit page, complete your profile:

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Display name** | The name shown on the streamers listing. |
    | **Twitch username** | Your Twitch channel name (if streaming on Twitch). |
    | **YouTube channel ID** | Your YouTube channel ID (if streaming on YouTube). |
    | **Headline** | A short description of your stream. |
    | **Description** | Longer description of your content and schedule. |

  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a profile picture">
    Go to [lidraughts.org/streamer/picture](https://lidraughts.org/streamer/picture) to upload a photo or avatar for your streamer listing. Click **Upload** and select an image file from your device.

    To remove your current picture, click **Delete picture** on the same page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Request approval">
    Once your profile is ready, click **Request approval** on the edit page. This sends your application to the Lidraughts streamer moderators.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Approval process

After you submit an approval request, a Lidraughts moderator with the `Streamers` permission reviews your profile. They can approve (list) or reject your application.

<Info>
  Moderators can also toggle whether your stream is **featured**, which gives it extra prominence on the listing page and in the featured API endpoint.
</Info>

Approval status changes are logged in the moderation log. You can check the current state of your profile at any time on [lidraughts.org/streamer/edit](https://lidraughts.org/streamer/edit).

<Warning>
  Lidraughts may remove a streamer's listing if their content violates the terms of service or community guidelines.
</Warning>

## Editing your streamer profile

You can update your streamer profile at any time at [lidraughts.org/streamer/edit](https://lidraughts.org/streamer/edit). Changes to Twitch or YouTube handles, your headline, and description take effect immediately on save. You do not need to re-apply for approval after editing, unless the platform requires re-review for specific fields.

Moderators can also edit any streamer's profile by appending `?u=<username>` to the edit URL.
