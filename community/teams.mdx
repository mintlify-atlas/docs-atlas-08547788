Teams are player-created groups on Lidraughts. They have their own pages, forums, member chats, and tournaments. Anyone can create a team or join an existing one.

## Browsing teams

Visit [lidraughts.org/team](https://lidraughts.org/team) to see the most popular teams. If you are already a member of one or more teams, the page redirects to your personal team list at `lidraughts.org/team/me`. You can browse all teams at `lidraughts.org/team/all` or search by name.

## Creating a team

<Info>
  You can create at most one team per week. If you have already created a team recently, the form will be blocked until the cooldown expires.
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the creation form">
    Go to [lidraughts.org/team/new](https://lidraughts.org/team/new) while logged in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the details">
    Provide the following fields:

    | Field | Requirements |
    |---|---|
    | **Name** | 3–60 characters. Must be unique across all teams. |
    | **Location** | Optional. 3–80 characters. |
    | **Description** | 30–4 000 characters. Shown on the team page. |
    | **Membership** | Open (anyone can join immediately) or approval required (leader reviews each request). |

  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the captcha">
    Solve the draughts captcha to confirm you are human.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Create** to publish the team. You are automatically added as the first member and become the team leader.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Joining a team

How you join depends on the team's membership setting.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Open team">
    Click **Join** on the team's page at `/team/<id>`. You are added instantly with no approval required.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Approval required">
    Click **Request membership** to open the join-request form at `/team/<id>/request/new`. Write a message (30–2 000 characters) explaining why you want to join. The team leader reviews your request and accepts or declines it. You receive a notification with the decision.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  If you change your mind before a leader acts on your request, visit the team page and click **Cancel request**.
</Tip>

## Team page

A team page at `/team/<id>` shows:

- Team name, location, and description
- Member count and member list (paginated)
- Live team chat (if enabled by the leader)
- Upcoming and recent tournaments
- Links to the team forum

## Team forum

Each team has a dedicated forum category. Team members can start topics and post replies. The forum is accessible from the team page and follows the same rules as the global [Forum](/community/forum).

<Note>
  Team forum categories are only visible to team members. Non-members cannot read or post in a team's private forum.
</Note>

## Team tournaments

Teams can host tournaments restricted to their members, including **Team Battles** where multiple teams compete against each other.

Visit `/team/<id>/tournaments` to see all tournaments associated with a team.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Team tournament" icon="trophy">
    A standard Arena or Swiss tournament where only team members can participate.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Team Battle" icon="swords">
    Multiple teams are pitted against each other. Each participant plays for their team, and the team with the most points wins.
  </Card>

Leaders can also send a message to all subscribed team members from the **PM all** admin page — useful for announcing upcoming events.

## Team leaders

The player who creates a team is its leader. A leader can:

- Edit the team's name, description, location, membership policy, and chat setting
- Accept or decline join requests
- Kick members from the team
- Transfer ownership to another member
- Send a broadcast message to all subscribed members

<Warning>
  Ownership transfer is permanent. Once you transfer ownership, you lose leader permissions unless the new owner grants them back.
</Warning>

To transfer ownership, go to `/team/<id>/changeOwner` and select a current member.

### Kicking a member

Leaders can remove members at `/team/<id>/kick`. Select the username from the list and confirm. The removed member can re-apply if the team has open membership.

## Leaving a team

To leave a team you belong to, open the team page and click **Quit team**. You will be redirected back to the team page and your membership is immediately removed.

<Note>
  The team leader cannot leave their own team without first transferring ownership to another member.
</Note>

## Member subscriptions

Team members can subscribe or unsubscribe from team messages. When a leader sends a broadcast via **PM all**, only subscribed members receive it. Toggle your subscription from the team page.

## Team search

Use the search bar at [lidraughts.org/team/search](https://lidraughts.org/team/search) to find teams by name. The search supports partial matches and returns results sorted by member count. Autocomplete is also available when adding a team reference elsewhere on the site.
