A Lidraughts account lets you build a rating history, join tournaments, save
games, and interact with the community. This page covers registration through
to securing your account.

## Registration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the sign-up page">
    Go to `https://lidraughts.org/signup` (`GET /signup`). The form is also
    reachable via the **Sign up** link in the top-right corner of any page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in your details">
    The sign-up form requires three fields:

    | Field | Rules |
    |---|---|
    | **Username** | Letters, digits, underscores, and hyphens. Between 2 and 20 characters. Case-insensitive for login but displayed as entered. |
    | **Email** | A valid, reachable email address. Used for confirmation and password recovery only — never shown publicly. |
    | **Password** | At least 4 characters. Choose something you do not reuse elsewhere. |

    Complete the CAPTCHA challenge if prompted, then click **Create my account**.

    The form is submitted to `POST /signup`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm your email">
    Depending on Lidraughts's server-side heuristics (new IP address, missing
    browser fingerprint, mobile signup), you may be redirected to a
    **Check your email** page at `GET /signup/check-your-email`.

    Open the confirmation email and click the link. The link hits:

    ```text
    GET /signup/confirm/:token
    ```

    On success you are automatically logged in and redirected to your new
    profile page. If you entered the wrong email address, use the fix-email
    form at `POST /signup/fix-email` before the token expires.
  </Step>

  <Step title="You're in">
    After confirmation (or immediately if email confirmation was not required)
    you land on your profile at `/@/:username`. Your account starts with no
    rating — you will be provisionally rated after your first games in each
    time-control category.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Profile setup

Navigate to **Settings → Profile** (`GET /account/profile`) to fill in your
public profile. Changes are saved with `POST /account/profile`.

Available profile fields:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Bio** | Free-text description, visible on your public profile. |
| **First name / Last name** | Optional real name. |
| **Country / Location** | Flag and region shown on your profile. |
| **FIDE rating** | Your official FIDE draughts rating, if you have one. |
| **Links** | Up to four external profile links (e.g. social media). |

Your username can also be changed once from **Settings → Username**
(`GET /account/username`), subject to availability.

## Preferences

Go to **Settings → Preferences** (`GET /account/preferences/:categSlug`) to
customise how Lidraughts looks and behaves. Preferences are applied globally
across all devices when you are signed in.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Display">
    Control the visual appearance of the board and site.

    | Setting | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Board theme** | The colour scheme of the draughts board squares. Set via `POST /pref/set/theme`. |
    | **Piece set** | The style of the draughts pieces. Set via `POST /pref/set/pieceSet`. |
    | **Background** | Light, dark, or a custom background image. Set via `POST /pref/set/bg` and `POST /pref/set/bgImg`. |
    | **Zen mode** | Hides most UI chrome during a game. Set via `POST /pref/set/zen`. |
    | **Board zoom** | Adjusts the board size. Stored as a session cookie via `POST /pref/set/zoom`. |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Sound">
    | Setting | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Sound set** | The sound theme for move notifications, clock warnings, and game events. Set via `POST /pref/set/soundSet`. |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Game behaviour">
    Game behaviour preferences — such as move confirmation, premove, and
    animation speed — are available in the full preferences form under the
    relevant category slugs (e.g. `/account/preferences/game`).
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Language">
    Lidraughts is available in 27 languages. Select your preferred language from
    **Settings** or via the language picker in the site footer. The selection is
    saved with `POST /translation/select`.

    If your language is missing or incomplete, you can contribute translations
    at [Crowdin](https://crowdin.com/project/lidraughts).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Two-factor authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a time-based one-time password (TOTP)
requirement at login, protecting your account even if your password is
compromised.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the 2FA settings page">
    Go to **Settings → Security → Two-factor authentication**
    (`GET /account/twofactor`). If 2FA is not yet active you will see the
    setup form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open any TOTP authenticator app (e.g. Google Authenticator, Authy, or a
    password manager with TOTP support) and scan the QR code shown on screen.
    The app will start generating 6-digit codes that rotate every 30 seconds.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify and save">
    Enter the current 6-digit code from your app into the confirmation field,
    then submit the form (`POST /account/twofactor/setup`). Lidraughts
    immediately closes all other active sessions to prevent unauthorised access.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To disable 2FA later, return to `GET /account/twofactor` and submit your
current TOTP code via `POST /account/twofactor/disable`.

<Info>
  Resetting your password also disables 2FA. Re-enable it after a password
  reset if your account requires it.
</Info>

## Kid mode

Kid mode restricts communication features — chat messages, forum posts, and
direct messages are disabled. It is intended for children or players who want
a distraction-free experience.

Toggle kid mode at **Settings → Kid mode** (`GET /account/kid`). The setting
is saved with `POST /account/kid`. You can also read and write the kid status
via the API:

```text
GET  /api/account/kid        # returns { "kid": true|false }
POST /api/account/kid?v=1    # enable
POST /api/account/kid?v=0    # disable
```

## OAuth token creation

Personal access tokens allow scripts and third-party tools to interact with
your account through the Lidraughts API without sharing your password.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the token page">
    Go to **Settings → API access tokens** (`GET /account/oauth/token`).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new token">
    Click **Generate a personal access token** (`GET /account/oauth/token/create`)
    and fill in:

    - **Description** — a label so you remember what the token is for.
    - **Scopes** — tick only the permissions your application needs. Available
      scopes include reading preferences, reading/writing games, managing
      challenges, and more.

    Submit the form (`POST /account/oauth/token/create`). The token is shown
    **once** — copy it immediately and store it somewhere safe.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use the token">
    Pass the token in the `Authorization` header for API requests:

    ```bash
    curl https://lidraughts.org/api/account \
      -H "Authorization: Bearer your_token_here"
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

To revoke a token, click **Delete** next to it on the token list page. The
delete route is `POST /account/oauth/token/:id/delete`.

## Account security

Review and manage active login sessions at **Settings → Security**
(`GET /account/security`). The page lists every active session with its
approximate location and device information.

To sign out a specific session, click **Revoke** next to it. To sign out
everywhere except your current session:

```text
POST /account/signout/all
```

This is useful if you logged in on a shared computer and forgot to sign out.

## Closing your account

<Warning>
  Closing your account is irreversible. Your username, games, and all associated
  data will be permanently removed and the username will not be available for
  re-registration.
</Warning>

If you still want to proceed, go to **Settings → Close account**
(`GET /account/close`), enter your password to confirm, and submit the form
(`POST /account/closeConfirm`). You will be signed out immediately.
