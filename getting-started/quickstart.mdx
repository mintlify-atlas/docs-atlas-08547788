<Note>
  No account is needed to play on Lidraughts. You can jump straight into a game
  anonymously from the lobby.
</Note>

Lidraughts supports both 10x10 and 8x8 draughts boards, with variants including
Frisian, Antidraughts, Frysk!, Breakthrough, Russian, and Brazilian draughts.
This guide walks you through finding and playing your first game.

## Playing without an account

Visit the lobby at `https://lidraughts.org/` (`GET /`). You can create and join
games immediately as a guest. Anonymous games use a session cookie to link you
to your side of the board — no registration required.

Guest players can:
- Create open challenges (hooks) in the lobby
- Accept challenges from other players
- Play against the computer (`/setup/ai`)
- Challenge a specific friend by URL

Some features — rated games, tournaments, and correspondence games — require a
registered account.

## Starting a game

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lobby">
    Go to `https://lidraughts.org/`. The lobby shows open game seeks from other
    players in real time. You can filter by time control, variant, and rated or
    casual mode using the **Filter** panel.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a game">
    Click **Create a game** to open the game setup dialog. Configure your
    preferred options:

    **Variant** — choose from:
    - **Standard** (10x10, international rules)
    - **Frisian** (10x10 with capturing kings)
    - **Antidraughts** (force your opponent to capture all pieces)
    - **Frysk!** (starts from a 5-piece setup)
    - **Breakthrough** (first to promote wins)
    - **Russian** (8x8, kings fly)
    - **Brazilian** (8x8, similar to Russian)

    **Time control** — one of three modes:

    | Mode | Description |
    |---|---|
    | Real-time | Both players share a clock. Enter minutes + increment in seconds. |
    | Correspondence | Days per turn (1–7). Moves are made asynchronously. |
    | Unlimited | No clock. Casual only. |

    **Mode** — **Casual** or **Rated**. Rated games require an account and affect
    your rating. Rated unlimited games are not allowed.

    **Color** — **White**, **Black**, or **Random**.

    Click **OK** to post the game as an open seek in the lobby. Other players
    will see it and can accept it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Join a game from the lobby">
    Open seeks are shown as tiles in the lobby. Each tile displays the
    requester's username (or "Anonymous"), their rating, the time control, and
    the variant. Click any tile to accept the game and be redirected to the
    board.

    The lobby WebSocket at `GET /lobby/socket/v:apiVersion` keeps the list live
    — no page refresh needed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Play the game">
    Once both players are connected the game starts. The board URL follows this
    pattern:

    ```text
    # Spectator view (watcher)
    GET /{gameId}/{color}
    # e.g. https://lidraughts.org/aB3cD4eF/white

    # Player view (your turn — uses a 12-character full ID)
    GET /{fullId}
    # e.g. https://lidraughts.org/aB3cD4eFwXyZ
    ```

    The `gameId` is 8 characters; the `fullId` appends a 4-character player
    token. Sharing the `gameId/{color}` URL lets anyone spectate without being
    able to move pieces.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the game interface

During a game you will see:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Board" icon="grid-2x2">
    The draughts board, oriented from your perspective. Captured pieces appear
    off to the side. Move animations and legal-move highlights are on by default.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Clock" icon="clock">
    Both players' remaining time. Your clock counts down only when it is your
    turn. The increment is added to your clock after each move.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Move list" icon="list">
    All moves in PDN notation. Click any move to replay the position up to that
    point without leaving the game.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Chat" icon="message-square">
    An in-game chat visible to both players and spectators. Spectators have a
    separate chat channel.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Basic controls

### Resigning

To resign, click the **Resign** button (flag icon) below the board and confirm
the prompt. The route that handles the resignation is:

```text
POST /{fullId}/resign
```

This immediately ends the game and grants the win to your opponent.

### Offering a draw

Click the **½** (draw offer) button to send a draw offer to your opponent. They
can accept or decline. If accepted, the game ends as a draw. You cannot offer a
draw again immediately after your opponent declines.

### Playing against the computer

For offline practice, go to `https://lidraughts.org/setup/ai` (`GET /setup/ai`).
Choose a variant and the computer level (powered by the
[Scan 3.1](https://github.com/rhalbersma/scan) engine for all 10x10 variants).
The game starts immediately — no waiting for an opponent.

### Challenging a specific player

You can send a direct challenge to any user. The challenge is created with:

```text
POST /api/challenge/:user
```

Or visit `https://lidraughts.org/@/:username` and click **Challenge** on their
profile. The other player receives a notification and can accept or decline.
